Personal Information Privacy

Case Study: Diksha App Data Exposure

What exactly happened and what were the consequences?

In 2023, India's Diksha education app, used by students and teachers during the pandemic, exposed personal data because a cloud server was left unprotected.

The data leak affected over 1 million teachers and almost 600,000 students

The exposed information included names, email addresses, phone numbers, school names, and enrollment details. Even though some details were partially hidden, it still allowed unauthorized people to piece together personal identities.


What hackers can do with your personal information

  • Steal your money
  • Commit identity theft
  • Access your accounts
  • Sell your information on the dark web
  • Use your data for phishing attacks
  • Harass or blackmail you
  • Damage your reputation
  • Target you with spam or scams
  • Gain unauthorized access to your devices
  • Use your information for illegal activities
  • Impersonate you online

How hackers steal your personal information

  • Unusual or misspelled domain names (Eg: gogle.com)
  • Links that do not match the displayed text
  • Links shared by unknown or untrusted sources
  • Links with unusual file extensions (Eg: .exe, .scr)
  • Shortened Links (Eg: bit.ly/2kT3sY)
  • Links that ask for personal information
  • Strange characters or symbols (Eg: faceb00k.com)
  • Extra words in the address (Eg: school-login.safe-access-verify.com)

How can you protect your information?

  1. Create strong passwords
  2. Use two-factor authentication
  3. Be cautious with sharing personal information online
  4. Regularly update your privacy settings on social media
  5. Keep your devices updated at all times
  6. Back up your data securely
  7. Be careful on public Wi-fi, try to avoid them in most cases
  8. Optional: Use a VPN for secure internet connections
